KOALA

Koalas are marsupials native to Australia, famous for their fluffy ears, button noses, and chilled-out lifestyle. Despite being called "koala bears," they aren’t bears at all — they’re closer to kangaroos and wombats.

These cuddly-looking creatures spend most of their time sleeping — up to 18–22 hours a day! When they're awake, they're usually busy munching on eucalyptus leaves, which are both their food and their water source.

Koalas live high up in the trees, gripping branches with their strong claws and opposable thumbs. They’re mostly solitary and communicate with deep grunts or squeaky baby-like calls during mating season.
