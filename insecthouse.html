INSECT HOUSE

The Insect House is a fascinating exhibit dedicated to some of nature’s smallest yet most extraordinary creatures. From leafcutter ants and beetles to butterflies and tarantulas, this place is crawling (literally) with life!

Insects make up the largest group of animals on Earth, with millions of species doing everything from pollinating flowers to breaking down dead matter. Despite their tiny size, insects play a HUGE role in ecosystems around the world.

Visitors can explore live colonies, see insects at work, and even walk through a butterfly dome where colorful wings flap all around you. It's weird, wild, and way more fun than you’d expect!

WHERE WILL YOU FIND IT

The Insect House is located in zone B8, tucked between the reptile garden and the botanical walk.
